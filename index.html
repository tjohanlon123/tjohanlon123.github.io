About Me

I am a small town boy from Pendleton, Oregon. Growing up, I was always fascinated with how things worked and the physical principles that drived them. This was especially true when it came to objects in space. My fascination drove me to study physics and astrophysics at UC Berkeley. At UC Berkeley, I loved exploring the new things and experinces that the Bay Area had to offer. From camping and backpacking around California to playing on the Berkeley Quidditch team, I truly enjoyed my time at Berkeley. I even developed professionaly by working as a researcher at Space Science Lab. Berkeley taught me so much and I am exited for what the future holds!

Programming

Having grown up with computers my whole life, I have always been comfortable using them. Throughout middle school and high school, I learned how to use Microsoft Word, Excel, and Powerpoint and have continued to develope these skills in college. I also got introduced to CAD software and basic programming and gained some practice with it in high school robotics club.

My freshman year of college, I took my first course on Python and scientific programming. My sophomore year, I further developed my programming classes and through this developement, I was able to gain my research position at Space Science Lab. Through a combination of work as a research intern and my coursework, I have been programming in python for over 4 years.

Using python, I have developed programs for image and signal processing, wrote GUIs to control motors and operating CCD camera, made programs utilizing bayesian statistics, machine learning, and regression. I am familiar with many packages in Python such as NumPy, pandas, matplotlib, astropy, george, pymc3, sklearn and more.

Beyond just python, I have developed skills in other programming languages as well. In my Data structures course, we learned and used Java throughout the course as well as Git and how to use Github. In my coursework, we would often have to do lab write ups and papers in LaTeX. At Space Science Lab, I learned how to use IDL, Labview and basic unix and linux. I have also learned C++, SQL, and HTML and CSS throughout my time at Berkeley.
